Student Success Tips

Practical advice from experienced students to help you navigate college life

Develop a Study System

Create a consistent study routine rather than cramming. Study in shorter, focused sessions with breaks in between. Use active learning techniques like summarizing, teaching concepts to others, or creating flashcards.

Academic

Use a Planner

Whether digital or paper, a planner is essential for tracking assignments, exams, and deadlines. Review your planner daily and plan your week every Sunday evening to stay on top of your responsibilities.

Time Management

Form Study Groups

Connect with classmates to form study groups. Explaining concepts to others reinforces your own understanding, and you'll benefit from different perspectives. Keep groups focused and productive.

Academic Social

Prioritize Sleep

Aim for 7-9 hours of quality sleep each night. Sleep is crucial for memory consolidation and cognitive function. Establish a consistent sleep schedule, even on weekends, for better academic performance.

Wellness

Break Down Large Projects

Divide major assignments into smaller, manageable tasks with their own deadlines. This prevents procrastination and reduces stress. Use the "chunking" method to tackle complex projects systematically.

Time Management

Build Relationships with Professors

Attend office hours, participate in class, and show genuine interest in the subject. These relationships can lead to research opportunities, strong recommendation letters, and valuable mentorship.

Social Academic

Maintain a Balanced Diet

Don't rely solely on fast food and snacks. Incorporate fruits, vegetables, and protein into your meals. Meal prepping on weekends can help you eat healthier during busy weeks.

Wellness

Find Your Ideal Study Spots

Experiment with different environments to discover where you study most effectively. Options include the library, quiet coffee shops, empty classrooms, or dedicated study rooms.

Academic

Get Involved on Campus

Join clubs, organizations, or intramural sports that interest you. Campus involvement helps you build community, develop leadership skills, and create a more balanced college experience.

Social

Use the Pomodoro Technique

Work in focused 25-minute intervals followed by 5-minute breaks. After four cycles, take a longer 15-30 minute break. This technique can improve focus and prevent burnout during long study sessions.

Time Management

Incorporate Physical Activity

Regular exercise reduces stress, improves mood, and boosts energy. Even 30 minutes of activity most days can make a significant difference. Use campus recreation facilities or simply take walking breaks between classes.

Wellness

Utilize Campus Resources

Take advantage of tutoring centers, writing labs, career services, and academic advisors. These resources are included in your tuition and can significantly support your academic success.
